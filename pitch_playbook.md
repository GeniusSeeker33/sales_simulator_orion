## 2-Minute Pitch — Distributor VP of Sales
*(Use this in the Orion exec meeting and any distributor sales leader conversation)*

**Open with their pain:**

"How long does it take one of your new reps to get to full productivity? In distribution, we typically see 12 to 18 months before someone is consistently carrying their number. And in that window, they're learning on your actual dealer relationships. Every botched call to a store owner is a relationship that costs you future orders, not just today's order.

The problem isn't that your reps aren't willing to work. It's that there's no safe place to fail. You can role-play with a manager for 30 minutes before a call, but that doesn't scale across 40 reps, and most managers don't have time for it anyway.

**What we built:**

SalesSimulator puts an AI dealer on the other end of the line. Your rep picks the account type — skeptical store owner, price shopper, dormant dealer who stopped buying eight months ago — and picks how hard they want it. The AI responds the way a real dealer would: it pushes back on price, it brings up your competitor's catalog, it cuts the call short if it's not going somewhere useful. When the call ends, the system scores exactly what the rep did well and what they missed. Not a human opinion — a structured rubric against the same standard every time.

Your managers see every rep's progress on a dashboard. You can see who's improving and who isn't, and you have a coaching conversation based on data instead of gut feel.

**Why this matters for firearms specifically:**

Your reps need to know more than a generic sales methodology. They need to know your catalog, your programs, and how to navigate the conversations that are unique to this industry — allocation, MAP compliance, FFL renewal situations. We're building those scenarios specifically for distribution. We already have the comp plan logic for Orion's structure built in, so reps can see exactly how their training performance maps to their earnings trajectory.

**Where we are:**

We're live inside Orion right now, and we're looking for two or three distribution partners to run a 90-day pilot. The deal is simple: your reps get access, you give us honest feedback on what the scenarios are missing, and at the end you decide if it's worth paying for. If it doesn't measurably improve your new rep ramp time, you don't owe us anything."

## 2-Minute Pitch — Licensing Partner in an Adjacent Vertical
*(For a Director of Sales Operations at a spirits distributor, medical device manufacturer, or large automotive parts distributor)*

**Open with their pain:**

"How many sales training programs have you run where you paid for the content, your team sat through it, and three weeks later you couldn't tell if anything changed? Most sales training fails for a predictable reason: adults don't learn by watching videos or reading case studies. They learn by doing something, getting feedback, and trying again. The problem is that 'doing' sales requires another human in the room, and that doesn't scale.

That's the gap we close.

**What we built:**

SalesSimulator is an AI roleplay platform for complex, technical B2B sales. Your rep sits down and has a real conversation with an AI that behaves like your actual buyers — whatever type of buyer is hardest for your team. The AI doesn't give up early. It objects, compares you to competitors, asks questions your rep doesn't have good answers to. When the call ends, your rep gets a scorecard with specific feedback: what they said that worked, what they missed, and exactly what they should have said instead.

We built this inside a regulated distribution business, which means we already understand the dynamics that make your sales environment difficult: compliance language your reps have to navigate carefully, deep product knowledge buyers expect on the first call, and relationship-based selling where one bad conversation damages a partnership worth hundreds of thousands of dollars per year.

**Why licensing makes sense:**

We are not trying to build a generic sales training platform. We're building industry-specific simulation engines. That means we can white-label the underlying platform with your branding and build a custom scenario library for your specific buyers, your specific objections, and your specific compliance requirements. Your training team defines what good looks like — we build the AI that enforces it at scale.

The licensing structure is straightforward: platform fee plus per-seat, annual contract, with a 90-day pilot for your first cohort to validate fit before you commit the full team.

**The ask:**

If you have 20 reps you'd let us run a pilot with, we can have a custom scenario library ready in 30 days. The only thing I need from your side is one sales manager who'll spend two hours with us describing what your worst calls sound like — that's what we train the AI on."

## Usage Notes

**For the Orion exec meeting specifically:**

- Lead with the recruiter angle: you built this because you've watched reps struggle in the same patterns during onboarding, and you saw that coaching wasn't scaling.
- Do not demo voice mode until you've tested it — it may not work.
- Pre-load 2–3 completed simulator sessions before the demo so the leaderboard and history look real.
- Fix the broken API calls before any live demo.
- When CFO asks about ROI: frame it as reducing new rep attrition and shortening time-to-productivity. If Orion loses one $80k-salary rep in year one because they weren't ramping, that's $40k+ in recruiting cost plus the lost revenue. If this tool gets one rep productive two months earlier, the math works.

**What to avoid saying:**

- "Revolutionary" — it isn't, and sophisticated buyers will know it.
- "AI-powered" as a standalone claim — everything is AI-powered in 2026; say specifically what the AI does.
- Specific integration capabilities (BC, FedEx, RingCentral) unless asked — they don't work yet.
- Exact price before the pilot conversation — anchor to the problem's cost first.
